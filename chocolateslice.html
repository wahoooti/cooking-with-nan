Nan's Chocolate Slice

Blink and you'll miss it! This family favourite is sure to be gone in seconds.

First to make the biscuit base

  1. Preheat the oven to 200°C.
  2. Grease a baking tin with margarine, then line with baking paper.
  3. Melt the margarine in a bowl in the microwave.
  4. Put the weetbix into a plastic bag and roll over with a rolling pin until they are finely crushed.
  5. In a large bowl, combine the crushed weetbix, flour, brown sugar and coconut.
  6. Pour in the cooled butter until well combined.
  7. Spread the mixture into the pan and lightly press down on the mixture without flattening it too much. You want it to have slight irregularities and be roughly 1.5cm high. If you have added too much butter or the mixture feels too wet, you can sprinkle extra designated coconut on the top to absorb some of the moisture.
  8. Put slice into the oven for 35 minutes or until golden brown on top.

Now to make the icing

  1. While baking make the chocolate icing by mixing the icing sugar and sieving coco powder into a bowl.
  2. Once combined slowly add the water. You want the icing to be a little more on the runny side than you would normally have for a cake.
  3. Once the slice is golden brown on top, remove from the oven and pour the icing ontop while it’s hot, to be about half a cm thick.

Ingredients

For the slice

125g margarine

4 weetbix

1 cup of brown sugar

1 cup of self-raising flour

½ cup of desicated coconut

For the Icing

2 cups of icing sugar

2 dsp Bournville Cocoa Powder

½ cup water

What You'll Need

Rolling pin

30cm x 20cm baking tin

Baking paper
