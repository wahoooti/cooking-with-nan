Chocolate Slice

Blink and you'll miss it! This family favourite is sure to be gone in seconds.

First to make the biscuit base

  1. Preheat the oven to 150°C.
  2. Grease a baking tin with margarine, then line with baking paper.
  3. Melt the margarine in a bowl in the microwave and set aside.
  4. Put the weetbix into a plastic bag and roll over with a rolling pin until finely crushed.
  5. In a large bowl, combine the crushed weetbix, flour, brown sugar and coconut.
  6. Stir in the cooled butter until combined.
  7. Spread the mixture into the pan and lightly press down on the mixture without flattening too much. You want slight irregularities and be roughly 1.5cm high. If the mixture feels too wet, you can sprinkle extra desiccated coconut on the top to absorb some of the moisture.
  8. Put slice into the oven for 35 minutes or until golden brown on top.

Now to make the icing

  1. While baking make the chocolate icing by mixing the icing sugar and sieved coco powder into a bowl.
  2. Once combined slowly add the water. You want the icing to be a little more on the runny side than you would normally have for a cake.

To serve

  1. Once the slice is golden brown on top, remove from the oven and pour the icing ontop while it’s hot, to be about half a cm thick.
  2. Leave to cool and keep a far distance from from Brother Ben.

To store

This slice can also be frozen and left to defrost in the fridge. It has also been known to be eaten straight from the freezer for those that can’t wait.


Ingredients

For the slice

150g margarine

4 weetbix

1 cup of brown sugar

1 cup of self-raising flour

½ cup of desiccated coconut

For the Icing

2 cups of icing sugar

2 tbs Bournville Cocoa Powder

½ cup water

What You'll Need

Rolling pin

30cm x 20cm baking tin

Baking paper
